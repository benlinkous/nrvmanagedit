## What Is an Endpoint?

An endpoint is any device we actively manage — desktop computers, laptops, or workstations used by staff or volunteers. Each device is priced individually so costs stay transparent and proportional to actual usage.

## What's Not Included

<ul class="p-checklist">
  <li><span class="p-check">–</span> On-site visits</li>
  <li><span class="p-check">–</span> Hardware purchases or repairs</li>
  <li><span class="p-check">–</span> Large projects or major migrations</li>
  <li><span class="p-check">–</span> Issues involving unmanaged or unsupported devices</li>
</ul>

If work falls outside standard scope, we discuss options before any additional effort is performed. No surprise charges.

## Why This Model Works

Flat per-endpoint pricing keeps your monthly costs predictable, scales easily as your organization grows, and aligns support effort with actual device count. Proactive management reduces emergencies — which means fewer unexpected costs over time.
